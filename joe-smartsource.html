Call Cheatsheet

SmartSource — IT/AV Technical Specialist
Company
SmartSource (ABCOM Technology Group)
Role
IT/AV Technical Specialist · Req 1209
Location
Englewood, NJ + 3 others · 70% travel (~20-25 events)
Full-time Path to PM Regional

Before You Dial

  • Have your resume open in front of you
  • Know who recommended you — be ready to name them
  • Pick your strongest area (audio / video / LED / IT)

Your 3 Strengths

  • 1No-ego generalist — push cases, drive trucks, climb ladders, stay for troubleshooting
  • 2Second career energy — came in with transferable skills, hungry to grow, not jaded
  • 3Range of event types — not pigeonholed, adaptable on-site

Their 3 Priorities

  • Independent on-site execution in a specialty area
  • Support sales with technically sound proposals
  • Someone growing toward Project Management
Connect your stuff to theirs: your range + work ethic = reliable on-site. Second career skills = client-facing polish. Wanting to specialize = PM trajectory.

The Script

Opening
"Hi, my name is Joe [Last Name] — I was recommended to reach out about the IT/AV Technical Specialist position, Requisition 1209. Do you have a couple minutes, or is there a better time?"
If they ask who recommended
"[Name] suggested I get in touch. They thought my background would be a good fit for what you're building out regionally."
The Pitch (~45 sec, keep conversational)
"I've been working in events for a couple years now and I'm a generalist — audio, video, LED, IT, I've touched all of it across a range of event types. I'm the guy who's happy to push cases, pack a truck, drive it to the venue, get up on a ladder, and then stay to troubleshoot when something goes sideways. I don't have a line between 'my job' and 'not my job' on site.

AV is actually a second career for me, which I think works in my favor — I came in with a whole set of skills from before and it's made me a better tech. Problem-solving, working with clients, staying calm when things go fast. Coming into this industry has honestly been one of the most exciting things I've done, and I want to keep going deeper. That's what drew me to this role — the chance to specialize and grow toward PM."
The Ask
"I know the listing mentions three years — I'm close but not quite there on paper. What I do bring is range, a strong work ethic, and the fact that I don't need hand-holding on a job site. Is that something worth a conversation, or is the three years a hard line?"
If yes
"Great — what works on your end? I can send over my resume today and make myself available whenever."
If they push back
"I totally understand. Would it make sense to submit my resume anyway so you have me on file? I'm not going anywhere in this industry and I'd love to be on your radar."
Close (either way)
"I appreciate your time. Is there a direct email I should send my resume to?"

The Experience Gap

They want 3 years. You have ~2. Don't hide it.

"I'm close but not quite there on paper."

Referral + attitude + willingness to do the unglamorous work covers the gap. If they push back, ask to stay on file.

Questions to Ask Them

  • "What does the ramp-up look like for new hires — is there a certification path?"
  • "Which regions are you staffing for right now?"
  • "What's the split between event types — corporate, tradeshow, entertainment?"
  • "What's the team structure on a typical event?"

Quick Reference

Do

  • Name the referral early
  • Say you're happy to travel
  • Show you know the role (specialty → PM)
  • Be warm and direct

Don't

  • Apologize for the experience gap
  • List every piece of gear
  • Ask about salary on this call
  • Ramble — pitch under a minute
Good luck, Joe.
